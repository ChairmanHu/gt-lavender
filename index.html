FAQ

What is Lavender Graduation?

Lavender Graduation is an event that brings together the entire student body, faculty, staff and alumni to celebrate the accomplishments of graduating lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, queer, intersex and ally students at the undergraduate and graduate levels.

Who gets recognized?

The Lavender Graduation recognizes lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender and ally students who are graduating. That includes undergraduate, masters and doctoral candidates.

Who is invited?

The Lavender Graduation is open to all students, faculty, staff and alumni who want to celebrate the accomplishments of these amazing students.

Why Lavender Graduation? Is it a separate event from the official Institute's graduation?

The Lavender Graduation is just another celebration, just as the official graduation is a celebration. It is on a smaller scale and allows alumni to participate and build strength and connections within the Georgia Tech community.

Why should I participate as a graduate?

Lavender Graduation rewards you for your accomplishments while being a student at Tech. It also serves as a formal "goodbye" to the community. You will be able to serve as an example and inspiration to the Tech community, particularly fellow students.

Who attends Lavender Graduation?

Everyone is welcome! Students, faculty, staff and alumni all attend the ceremony. If you have individuals you would like to invite to watch you participate, simply reserve enough spots on the RSVP form. We encourage you to invite faculty, staff and students who have contributed to your success at Tech. This is your opportunity to honor them as well.

Will I miss my "real" graduation?

No, you will not. Lavender Graduation is held in April, well before GT's official graduation.

Is there a fee?

There is no fee.

Do I have to be an LGBT leader or extensively involved with LGBT issues?

No, you do not. Lavender Graduation is held to celebrate all sorts of achievements by LGBT and ally students.

What should I wear to the ceremony?

Don't worry: no caps and gowns for this ceremony! Business casual is recommended but you are welcome to dress as you wish.

Who is hosting?

Pride Alumni, the Office of the Vice President for Institute Diversity, the Office of Student Diversity Programs, Pride Alliance and oSTEM are hosting.

How does Lavender Graduation contribute to the overall mission of GT?

The Lavender Graduation celebrates the academic achievements of Georgia Tech students, particularly those who are graduating. It celebrates inclusivity and diversity. According to the GT Strategic Plan, "We will recruit, develop, retain, and engage a diverse cadre of students, faculty, and staff with a wide variety of backgrounds, perspectives, interests, and talents, creating a campus community that exemplifies the best in all of us - in our intellectual pursuits, our diversity of thought, our personal integrity, and our inclusive excellence." Finally, it invites alumni to participate and strengthen bonds with the Institute.

Is this only for Pride Alliance? Who can come?

The Lavender Graduation is open to all students, faculty, staff and alumni, not just members of Pride Alliance or the Pride Alumni Affinity Group.

Who can be recognized?

All lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, queer, intersex and ally graduates are eligible to be recognized. May 2013 graduates, as well as August 2013 and Fall 2013 graduates, are eligible. Students should register by Wednesday, April 10, 2013.
